### Part 1: Research on Program Goals from Other Institutions

Here are examples of program-level goals (often called Program Learning Outcomes or PLOs) from leading universities and
a national skills framework. These will provide a solid foundation for what is considered essential in the field.

#### From Top Universities

1. **Stanford University - M.S. in Statistics: Data Science Track**
    * **Goals:** Students will have the ability to...
        * Translate a real-world problem into a statistical model.
        * Have a deep understanding of statistical and probabilistic concepts.
        * Master computational tools for data analysis.
        * Communicate statistical findings effectively in oral and written form.
        * Understand professional and ethical responsibilities.

2. **Carnegie Mellon University - Master of Science in Machine Learning**
    * **Goals:** Students will demonstrate an ability to...
        * Apply a broad range of machine learning models and algorithms.
        * Identify and formulate a machine learning problem from a real-world task.
        * Design and conduct experiments to evaluate machine learning models.
        * Function effectively as a member of a team.
        * Communicate technical knowledge precisely and effectively.

3. **UC Berkeley - B.A. in Data Science**
    * **Goals:** Students will be able to...
        * Formulate questions that can be addressed with data.
        * Use computational and inferential tools to answer questions.
        * Communicate results and conclusions effectively.
        * Critically engage with the societal and ethical implications of data.

#### From a National Skills Framework

4. **SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG) - Skills Framework for ICT (Data & AI)**
    * This framework lists **Critical Core Skills** alongside technical skills. These are effectively program goals for
      the entire national workforce.
        * **Thinking Critically:** Problem Solving, Transdisciplinary Thinking.
        * **Interacting with Others:** Collaboration, Communication, Influence.
        * **Staying Relevant:** Learning Agility, Self-Management.

**Common Themes from Research:**
Across these top-tier examples, a clear pattern emerges. A successful analytical professional must master four key
areas:

* **Problem Formulation & Modeling:** Translating real-world problems into technical ones.
* **Technical Execution & Rigor:** Applying the right tools and methods correctly.
* **Communication & Influence:** Explaining findings and their implications to others.
* **Ethics & Professionalism:** Understanding the context and responsibilities of their work.

### Part 2: Formulating Goals for Your DSAI Framework

Based on your competency map and the insights from the research, here is a retrospectively formulated set of program
goals. I have designed five distinct goals that map directly to your framework. For each one, I provide a rationale for
why it's essential and what it should and shouldn't include in a corporate context vs. an academic one.

---

#### **Proposed Goal 1: End-to-End Strategic Problem Solving**

* **Goal Statement:** "To equip data scientists with the ability to **diagnose complex business problems and lead the
  design of rigorous, end-to-end data science projects** that deliver meaningful and actionable outcomes."

* **Maps to Your Competencies:** `Data Project Design & Problem Formulation`.

* **Rationale (Why this is essential):** This is the ultimate "value" goal. A data scientist who can only execute
  technical tasks is a technician. One who can identify a business need, frame it as a soluble data problem, and design
  a plan to solve it is a strategic partner. This goal ensures that the work is always tied to business impact.

* **What We Should & Shouldn't Include:**
    * **We SHOULD focus on:** Practical scoping, risk identification, and translating ambiguous user needs (*
      *elicitation**) into concrete project plans. The focus is on delivering a solution.
    * **We SHOULDN'T focus on:** Purely theoretical research proposals or open-ended exploration without a clear
      business objective, which might be common in an academic Ph.D. program.

---

#### **Proposed Goal 2: Rigorous Data Analysis and Inference**

* **Goal Statement:** "To ensure data scientists can **transform raw data into reliable insights through a process of
  deft preparation, skeptical exploration, and the application of robust statistical inference.**"

* **Maps to Your Competencies:** `Data Preparation`, `Exploratory Data Analysis`, `Statistical Analysis`.

* **Rationale:** This is the "integrity" goal. It ensures that the conclusions we draw are defensible, validated, and
  based on a true understanding of the data. Without this rigor, data science can be dangerously misleading. It
  separates professional analysis from amateur data-poking.

* **What We Should & Shouldn't Include:**
    * **We SHOULD focus on:** Applied techniques for data cleaning, the practical validation of model assumptions (e.g.,
      checking for IID), and the correct interpretation of confidence/credible intervals.
    * **We SHOULDN'T focus on:** Deriving the mathematical proofs behind statistical tests or the deep theory of measure
      spaces, which would be central to a university statistics degree.

---

#### **Proposed Goal 3: High-Fidelity Predictive System Development**

* **Goal Statement:** "To enable data scientists to **build, evaluate, and explain high-performance predictive systems**
  using clean, efficient, and idiomatic code."

* **Maps to Your Competencies:** `Machine Learning`, `Code Fluency`.

* **Rationale:** This is the "builder" goal. While analysis provides one-off insights, predictive systems create
  scalable and repeatable value. This goal ensures our data scientists can create tangible assets (models, APIs) that
  can be integrated into the organization's operations.

* **What We Should & Shouldn't Include:**
    * **We SHOULD focus on:** Fluency with standard libraries (`scikit-learn`, `fastapi`), the ability to write
      maintainable and reproducible modeling code, and the skill to deploy a model as a simple service.
    * **We SHOULDN'T focus on:** Low-level, highly-optimized algorithm implementation from scratch or complex software
      engineering patterns (e.g., advanced distributed systems), which would be the domain of a dedicated Research
      Scientist or ML Engineer.

---

#### **Proposed Goal 4: Effective Communication and Influence**

* **Goal Statement:** "To develop data scientists who can **translate complex analytical findings into clear, compelling
  narratives** that are tailored to the audience and capable of driving stakeholder decisions."

* **Maps to Your Competencies:** `Visualisations & Data Storytelling`.

* **Rationale:** This is the "return on investment" goal. An insight has no value until it is understood and acted upon
  by someone who can make a decision. The ability to communicate effectively is the final, crucial step that converts
  analytical work into business impact.

* **What We Should & Shouldn't Include:**
    * **We SHOULD focus on:** Structuring presentations for management, designing clear and honest data visualizations,
      and the soft skill of anticipating audience questions and concerns.
    * **We SHOULDN'T focus on:** The academic style of conference presentations or writing dense, formal papers for
      peer-reviewed journals.

---

#### **Proposed Goal 5: Ethical and Collaborative Professional Conduct**

* **Goal Statement:** "To cultivate data scientists who operate with a **strong sense of ethical responsibility and
  actively contribute to the team and community** through mentorship, collaboration, and the promotion of best
  practices."

* **Maps to Your Competencies:** This goal gives a home to the crucial but often overlooked competencies of
  `Community Contribution`, `Technical Leadership`, and the ethical considerations woven throughout your framework.

* **Rationale:** This is the "sustainability and culture" goal. It ensures that the team operates in a way that is
  responsible, avoids ethical and reputational risks, and fosters a positive, collaborative environment where knowledge
  is shared and junior members can grow. A team without this goal will eventually stagnate or run into trouble.

* **What We Should & Shouldn't Include:**
    * **We SHOULD focus on:** Practical ethical frameworks for data science, active participation in internal tech
      sharings, and demonstrating mentorship within project teams.
    * **We SHOULDN'T focus on:** Deep philosophical debates on ethics. The goal is applied ethics and positive
      professional behavior.
