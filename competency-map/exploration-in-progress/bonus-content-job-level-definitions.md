(extended version of the definition from the slides, written with help from gemini)

|                     | L1: Task-Focused                                                   | L2: Project-Focused                                                     | L3: Outcome-Focused                                                                                                                     | L4: Initiative-Focused                                                                                                                     | L5: Strategy-Focused                                                                                                   |
|:--------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------|:------------------------------------------------------------------------|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Core Action         | Execute                                                            | Solve                                                                   | Architect                                                                                                                               | Orchestrate & Align                                                                                                                        | Author & Steer                                                                                                         |
| Outcome Focus       | Task Completion: Correctly completes assigned tasks.               | Project Success: Delivers a valuable, end-to-end project.               | Business Outcome: Ensures their project creates a measurable, positive change in organizational performance.                            | Cross-Functional Success: Ensures the success of a major initiative by aligning multiple teams, domains, and systems.                      | Organizational Vision: Ensures the long-term health, innovation, and strategic positioning of the entire organization. |
| Problem Scope       | Routine Tasks: A small, clearly defined piece of a larger project. | Well-Defined Projects: A standard, end-to-end project with known goals. | Complex, Domain-Specific Systems: Addresses ambiguous problems within a technical domain by architecting a major system or methodology. | Complex, Cross-Functional Initiatives: Leads major organizational programs that span multiple technical domains, teams, or business units. | Long-Term Organizational Direction: Defines the 3-5+ year vision and makes the case for major strategic bets.          |
| Thinking Level      | Procedural: "How do I do the steps?"                               | Conceptual: "Why is this the best standard method?"                     | Systemic (Domain): "How do all the parts of this domain-specific system fit together for a robust outcome?"                             | Systemic (Cross-Domain): "How do I align different teams, technologies, and business needs to make this entire initiative succeed?"        | Strategic: "What *should* we be doing in the next five years, and why?"                                                |
| Approach to Methods | Follows: Adheres strictly to established playbooks.                | Selects: Chooses the appropriate method from a known set of options.    | Designs: Designs the architecture for a major, domain-specific solution.                                                                | Integrates: Integrates disparate systems and synthesizes solutions across technical and business boundaries.                               | Defines: Establishes the guiding principles and long-term vision that inform all future methods and architectures.     |
| Required Guidance   | High: Needs regular supervision and explicit instructions.         | Low: Works autonomously on projects, a "safe pair of hands."            | Source of Guidance: Is a thought partner and mentor for their technical domain.                                                         | Source of Leadership: Provides technical leadership and vision for a major cross-functional program.                                       | Source of Vision: Provides strategic counsel to the organization's most senior leaders.                                |
| Primary Artifact    | A completed analysis or a working script.                          | An end-to-end solution or a robust predictive model.                    | An architecture document for a major domain-specific system, a methodology guide.                                                       | A cross-organizational program plan, a multi-year technical roadmap for an initiative.                                                     | An organizational technical strategy document that is *organization-centric and nationally-aware*.                     |
