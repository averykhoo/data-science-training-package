### Competency: Problem Formulation and Project Design

**Definition:** The ability to identify business opportunities, elicit user needs, design a rigorous research approach,
and formulate a feasible project plan to deliver value.

| PL      | Level Name                                            | Key Focus                                                                                                              |
|:--------|:------------------------------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **PL1** | **Structured Task Execution**                         | **Doing things right:** Following a pre-defined plan reliably.                                                         |
| **PL2** | **Independent Project Leadership**                    | **Doing the right things:** Designing and executing a standard project from start to finish.                           |
| **PL3** | **Complex Problem Architect**                         | **Architecting custom solutions** for complex, ambiguous problems.                                                     |
| **PL4** | **Exceptional Research Taste & High-Leverage Design** | **Knowing the next right things to do:** Defining the problem space and creating leverage for the entire organization. |

---

#### **PL1: Structured Task Execution**

* **Guiding Principle:** Follows the trail markers on a pre-defined path.
* **Description:** This level demonstrates reliability and comprehension within a pre-existing project structure. The
  individual is not expected to design the project, but to execute an assigned component flawlessly and understand its
  context.
* **Key Assessable Behaviors:**
    * **Articulates Scope:** Can clearly describe the specific objectives of their assigned task and how it contributes
      to the broader project goals.
    * **Follows Process:** Adheres to the team's established project lifecycle, version control practices, and
      documentation standards for all work.
    * **Delivers on Spec:** Reliably delivers completed sub-tasks that verifiably meet the requirements set by a senior
      team member.
    * **Identifies Blockers:** Proactively identifies and communicates when a task is blocked (e.g., by data access
      issues, unclear requirements) and seeks guidance in a timely manner.

---

#### **PL2: Independent Project Leadership**

* **Guiding Principle:** Draws a reliable map for a known destination.
* **Description:** This level demonstrates ownership. The individual can be trusted to manage a standard, well-scoped
  project from a clear business need to a successful conclusion with minimal supervision.
* **Key Assessable Behaviors:**
    * **Problem Translation:** Translates a stakeholder's business need into a tractable data science problem with
      specific, measurable objectives.
    * **Project Planning:** Independently develops and documents a comprehensive project plan, including key milestones,
      deliverables, timelines, and communication points.
    * **Risk Identification:** Identifies and documents potential risks (e.g., data quality, modeling challenges) and
      proposes concrete mitigation strategies upfront.
    * **Standard Experimental Design:** Designs and documents a standard validation or experimental framework (e.g., a
      properly powered A/B test, a robust cross-validation strategy) to measure the project's outcome.

---

#### **PL3: Strategic Problem Architect**

* **Guiding Principle:** Charts a course through an ambiguous, unmapped jungle.
* **Description:** This level demonstrates the ability to navigate complexity and ambiguity. The individual is no longer
  just managing projects; they are architecting solutions to complex strategic problems where the path forward is not
  clear.
* **Key Assessable Behaviors:**
    * **Problem Decomposition:** Diagnoses a complex, ambiguous, or systemic business problem, producing a clear
      analysis that decomposes it into a set of underlying root causes and key questions.
    * **Research Program Design:** Architects and documents a multi-stage research and development program, prioritizing
      a sequence of projects to address the problem comprehensively.
    * **Advanced Experimental Design:** Designs and justifies robust, novel, or quasi-experimental approaches (e.g.,
      using causal inference from observational data) when standard A/B testing is demonstrably not feasible.
    * **Strategic Judgment (Qualitative Assessment):** Can rigorously articulate and defend the reasoning behind their
      chosen strategic path versus several other viable alternatives they considered. The assessment here focuses on the
      quality and depth of their reasoning about the rejected paths.

---

#### **PL4: Exceptional Research Taste & High-Leverage Design**

* **Guiding Principle:** Invents a new form of navigation and identifies which new continents are worth exploring.
* **Description:** A person at this level has moved beyond executing projects. Their primary value lies in their expert
  judgment and "research taste," which they use to define the work of others and maximize the organization's return on
  its R&D efforts.
* **Key Observable Traits:**
    * **A "Nose for Value":** Possesses a well-honed intuition for the difference between a problem that is merely
      interesting and one that is truly important. They are sought out for their opinion on whether a problem is worth
      solving in the first place.
    * **Deep Problem Reframing:** Uses first-principles thinking to decompose a business request to its fundamental
      assumptions, often reframing it into a more powerful and general problem that yields more durable insights.
    * **Designing for Leverage:** The primary output of their design process is not a one-off solution but a "force
      multiplier." They architect projects that create high-leverage, reusable assets (e.g., canonical datasets,
      foundational modeling libraries, standardized methodologies) that accelerate future work for the entire
      organization.
    * **Defining the Research Frontier:** They can see and articulate a multi-project arc, effectively setting the
      research agenda for their domain. They have the intellectual integrity to rigorously argue *against* pursuing
      low-value work, even if it is popular or interesting.
