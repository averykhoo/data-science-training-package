### Competency: Specialization

#### Competency Description

Specialization is the deep and proficient application of advanced analytical methods to a specific technical domain (
e.g., Text, Images, Graphs). It represents a level of expertise that goes beyond the general application of statistics
and machine learning, enabling the practitioner to solve complex, domain-specific problems effectively.

To demonstrate this competency, a candidate **must** ground their specialized work in the principles and practices of at
least one of the core functional competencies: **Machine Learning** or **Statistical Analysis**. A specialization is
therefore defined by the intersection of a **technical domain** and a **methodological path**. Purely theoretical,
symbolic, or classical algorithmic work, while valuable, does not fulfill the requirements of this competency unless it
is directly used to build or validate a statistical or machine learning model.

**A Note on the "Specialization" Competency**

The `Specialization` competency is a key requirement for roles at L2 and above, reflecting the need for deep expertise
to solve complex problems. A specialization must be a well-defined area of technical depth that is relevant to the
organization's business needs. The choice of a specialization should be a deliberate discussion between a data scientist
and their manager, ensuring alignment with both career aspirations and team goals. While a candidate presents their
specialization, the tech panel's role is to validate the *exhibited level of proficiency (PL)* within that domain. The
panel will assess the candidate's ability to apply specialized techniques with the rigor and depth appropriate for the
target PL, as defined in this framework.

---

### Proficiency Levels in Specialization

The proficiency levels for Specialization measure the practitioner's ability to move from applying standard advanced
techniques to creating novel, generalizable capabilities within their chosen domain.

* **PL2 (Application & Analysis):** At this level, the specialist can **apply and analyze** known, state-of-the-art
  models and techniques to solve well-defined problems within their domain. They can select appropriate specialized
  tools, benchmark their performance against established baselines, and clearly articulate the results and limitations
  of their chosen approach. The focus is on the successful implementation and evaluation of existing advanced methods.

* **PL3 (Adaptation & Synthesis):** The specialist can **adapt and synthesize** methods to solve complex and ambiguous
  domain-specific problems where no standard solution exists. This involves modifying existing algorithms, chaining
  multiple techniques together, or designing a custom solution tailored to unique data or business constraints. They can
  rigorously evaluate the trade-offs between multiple advanced approaches and defend their bespoke design. The focus is
  on architecting effective, custom solutions.

* **PL4 (Invention & Generalization):** The specialist can **invent and generalize** new capabilities that create
  leverage for the entire organization. Their work moves beyond solving a single problem to creating a novel framework,
  a reusable library, or a foundational methodology that enables other teams to solve an entire class of domain-specific
  problems more efficiently. The focus is on creating high-leverage, "force-multiplier" assets.

### Proficiency Level 1 in Specialization (Hypothetical)

* **PL1 (Foundational Exploration & Application):** At this level, the practitioner can **follow guided tutorials to
  apply basic, well-known specialized tools** to a standard problem within a domain. They can successfully execute a
  pre-defined workflow using a specialized library and describe the immediate output. The focus is on building initial
  familiarity and demonstrating that they can make a specialized tool "work" in a controlled environment.

This contrasts with PL2, where the focus shifts to independently selecting, benchmarking, and analyzing the results of
such tools to solve a real business problem.
