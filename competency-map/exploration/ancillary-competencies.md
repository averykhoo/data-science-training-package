### Ancillary Competency: Technical Leadership

This competency is about the ability to make and influence sound technical decisions, guiding work towards quality,
robustness, and maintainability.

| PL  | Level Name                  | Key Behaviors and Scope                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
|:----|:----------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| PL1 | Adherence & Self-Management | Doing things the right way.<br>• Reliably applies the team's established technical standards (e.g., coding style, version control, testing) to their own work.<br>• Demonstrates ownership by seeing assigned tasks through to completion and proactively communicating status.                                                            |
| PL2 | Guidance & Application      | Explaining and applying the right way.<br>• Makes appropriate technical choices from a set of known, standard options for a given problem.<br>• Clearly explains technical concepts or decisions to peers or junior members.<br>• Guides others on well-defined technical tasks.                                                           |
| PL3 | Architecture & Mentorship   | Designing a better way when no standard exists.<br>• Architects a robust and elegant technical solution for a complex or ambiguous problem by analyzing trade-offs and synthesizing different approaches.<br>• Effectively mentors others through complex technical challenges, helping them improve their own judgment and design skills. |
| PL4 | Stewardship & Vision        | Defining what "a better way" means for everyone.<br>• Establishes new technical standards, frameworks, or best practices that measurably improve the quality and efficiency of the entire organization.<br>• Provides trusted, high-level technical vision that shapes the work of multiple teams.                                         |

### Ancillary Competency: Community Contribution

This competency is about participating in and cultivating the collective knowledge and culture of the technical
community.

| PL  | Level Name                    | Key Behaviors and Scope                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
|:----|:------------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| PL1 | Integration & Active Learning | Effectively receiving from the community.<br>• Successfully integrates into the team by learning and adopting community norms and processes.<br>• Asks well-researched questions and demonstrates the ability to learn effectively from mentors, documentation, and shared resources.                                                      |
| PL2 | Participation & Sharing       | Giving back to the community.<br>• Actively participates in technical discussions.<br>• Contributes valuable knowledge by presenting a clear and useful sharing session or creating reusable artifacts (e.g., documentation, code snippets).                                                                                               |
| PL3 | Facilitation & Mentorship     | Improving the community's ability to share.<br>• Identifies and addresses knowledge gaps by taking the initiative to create new learning materials or lead focused discussions.<br>• Effectively mentors others, adapting their approach to help them grow.<br>• Is a significant and reliable contributor to a community-wide initiative. |
| PL4 | Cultivation & Stewardship     | Building systems for community growth.<br>• Establishes and leads high-impact community programs (e.g., tech panels, training curriculums, special interest groups) that foster a self-sustaining culture of learning and excellence.                                                                                                      |

### Ancillary Competency: Code Fluency

The ability to use programming languages to create solutions that are not only functional but also
clear, maintainable, and idiomatic, making them effective tools for collaboration and long-term value.
This includes the effective consumption, design, and implementation of libraries, frameworks, and APIs.

| PL  | Level Name           | CEFR Analogy              | Core Behavior                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Definition & Scope                                                                                                                                                                            | Key Indicators & Assessable Behaviors                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
|:----|:---------------------|:--------------------------|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| PL1 | Functional Scripter  | A2: Elementary            | Executes a known procedure. Can write a script that correctly performs a specific, well-defined task, much like using basic phrases to order a coffee. The code *works*.                                                   | Writes functional, procedural scripts that achieve a specified, immediate goal. Code is correct but may be inefficient or hard to read.                                                       | <ul><li>Library Usage: Can successfully install and use functions from standard libraries (`pandas`, `requests`, `scikit-learn`).</li><li>API Usage: Can read API documentation and write code to successfully call an existing API to retrieve or send data.</li><li>Code Quality: Scripts are functional for the author's immediate use but may lack comments, clear structure, or adherence to style guides.</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| PL2 | Idiomatic Programmer | B2: Upper-Intermediate    | Solves a standard problem. Can write clear, maintainable code that follows community best practices and common patterns (idioms). A native speaker (a senior colleague) can read it and immediately understand the intent. | Writes clean, well-structured, and documented code that adheres to idiomatic patterns of the primary language and community best practices.                                                   | <ul><li>Idiomatic Code: Consistently uses language-specific idioms (e.g., comprehensions in Python instead of manual loops).</li><li>Framework Usage: Effectively uses the core APIs of major frameworks (e.g., `scikit-learn` Pipelines, `pandas` data manipulation).</li><li>Simple API Design: Can design and implement a simple, consistent RESTful API for their models or analyses, typically using a framework like FastAPI. The API is reliable and easy for others to consume.</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| PL3 | Fluent Designer      | C1: Advanced              | Navigates ambiguity. Can design and architect elegant, custom solutions for complex problems by skillfully combining different patterns and language features. They express complex ideas clearly and effectively in code. | Designs and builds robust, maintainable, and high-leverage assets. Exhibits "taste" in selecting tools, programming patterns, or language features to architect elegant and robust solutions. | <ul><li>Library/Framework Creation: Can design and build a reusable library (like `fastapi`) or package for use by other team members, following best practices for structure, testing, and documentation.</li><li>Advanced API Design: Can design and implement complex APIs, correctly applying advanced patterns such as asynchronous programming for performance, or adhering to rigorous design standards (like Google AIPs) for consistency at scale.</li><li>Taste & Judgment: Can articulate the trade-offs between different libraries or frameworks and justify their choices based on "Pythonic" (or "R-like") design philosophy, maintainability, and fitness for a specific task.</li><li>Advanced Design: Designs and builds solutions using advanced patterns like custom decorators, context managers, or asynchronous programming. Exhibits "taste" in selecting tools.</li></ul> |
| PL4 | System Author        | C2: Mastery / Proficiency | Invents a new capability. Doesn't just use the language, but shapes it for others. They "write the grammar books" by creating high-leverage libraries, frameworks, or new standards that empower the entire community.     | Conceives and designs fundamental, high-leverage tools, frameworks, or standards that are adopted across the organization or even by the public community.                                    | <ul><li>Fundamental Creation and Standardization: Invents or defines new standards for how systems interact (e.g., creating or contributing to data exchange formats like gRPC or Arrow).</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
